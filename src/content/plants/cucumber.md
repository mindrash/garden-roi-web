Cucumbers are prolific producers that can overwhelm even the most dedicated harvester. With proper trellising, a single plant needs only 1–2 square feet while yielding 8–10 pounds of cucumbers. Regular harvesting triggers even more production.

## Growing Tips

Direct sow cucumbers outdoors after the last frost when soil reaches 70°F, or start indoors 2–3 weeks before transplanting. Cucumbers have sensitive roots — use biodegradable pots if starting indoors.

### Trellising
Vertical growing is the secret to cucumber success. A trellis, cattle panel, or string system:
- Improves air circulation (reduces disease)
- Makes harvesting easier
- Keeps fruit straight and clean
- Saves significant ground space

### Pollination
Cucumbers have separate male and female flowers. Males appear first and are smaller; females have a tiny cucumber at the base. If early female flowers are dropping without setting fruit, bees may be absent — hand-pollinate with a small brush by transferring pollen from male to female flowers.

## Watering
Cucumbers are 95% water and need consistent moisture. Water stress causes bitter fruit. Mulch heavily to retain soil moisture and reduce the frequency of watering needed.

## Harvest Timing
**Harvest before cucumbers turn yellow.** Once yellow, they trigger the plant to scale back production. Check plants every 1–2 days during peak season — cucumbers grow surprisingly fast in heat. Slicing types are best at 6–8 inches; pickling types at 2–4 inches.

## Common Varieties
- **Straight Eight / Marketmore** — Classic slicing, reliable producers
- **Persian Mini** — Snack-sized, thin skin, excellent flavor
- **Lemon** — Round yellow cucumbers; mild, never bitter
- **Kirby** — Best for pickling; bumpy skin

## Pest & Disease Watch
- **Cucumber beetles:** Yellow/black striped beetles that transmit bacterial wilt. Use row cover early in season; remove once flowers appear for pollination.
- **Powdery mildew:** White coating on leaves late in season. Preventive spray of diluted neem oil helps.
