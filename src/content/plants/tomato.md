Tomatoes are the cornerstone of the home garden — and one of the highest ROI crops you can grow. A single well-tended plant can yield 10–15 pounds of fruit worth $30–$45 at grocery prices, from a package of seeds costing under $3.

## Growing Tips

Plant transplants outdoors after the last frost date when soil temperature reaches at least 60°F. Bury the stem deep — tomatoes root along their buried stem, which builds a stronger plant.

### Soil & Fertilizing
Tomatoes are heavy feeders. Amend soil with compost before planting and side-dress with a balanced fertilizer (10-10-10) every 3–4 weeks during the season. Once flowers appear, switch to a low-nitrogen, high-phosphorus fertilizer to encourage fruit set over foliage.

### Pruning
Remove suckers (the shoots that grow in the crotch between stem and branch) on indeterminate varieties to focus energy on fruit production. Determinates generally don't need suckering.

### Watering
Inconsistent watering causes blossom end rot and fruit cracking. Water deeply and consistently — a drip system or soaker hose works best. Mulch around plants to retain moisture.

## Pest & Disease Watch
- **Hornworms:** Large green caterpillars that can defoliate a plant quickly. Pick off by hand or use Bt (Bacillus thuringiensis).
- **Early blight:** Brown spots on lower leaves. Remove affected leaves and avoid overhead watering.
- **Aphids:** Blast off with water or use insecticidal soap.

## Harvest
Pick when fully colored and slightly soft to the touch. Tomatoes continue to ripen off the vine at room temperature — never refrigerate before cutting, as cold destroys flavor.
