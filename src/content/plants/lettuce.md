Lettuce is one of the fastest-return crops in the garden. With a "cut-and-come-again" harvest strategy, a single planting can provide salad greens for weeks. At $4–$6 per bag of mixed greens at the store, a row of lettuce can easily save $50–$100 over a season.

## Growing Tips

Lettuce is a cool-weather crop — it grows best when temperatures are 45–75°F. It bolts (goes to seed and turns bitter) quickly in heat. Plan for spring and fall harvests in most climates; in cool coastal areas, it can grow year-round.

### Succession Planting
Instead of planting all your seeds at once, sow a new small batch every 2–3 weeks. This gives you a continuous harvest through the season rather than a single glut.

### Direct Sow
Scatter seeds thinly on prepared soil, press gently into the surface (light helps germination), keep moist. Thin to 6–8 inches apart once seedlings emerge. Use the thinnings in salads — they're delicious.

### Shade in Summer
As temperatures rise, use shade cloth or plant lettuce near taller plants (like tomatoes) to extend the harvest by 2–3 weeks before bolting.

## Cut-and-Come-Again Harvesting
For looseleaf varieties, use scissors to cut outer leaves 1 inch above the crown. The plant regrows and you can harvest the same plant 3–5 times. For head types, harvest the whole head then pull the plant.

## Varieties
- **Butterhead (Boston/Bibb)** — Soft, sweet, buttery texture; small heads
- **Romaine** — Tall, substantial; great for Caesar salads; heat-tolerant
- **Looseleaf (Red/Green Leaf)** — Fastest growing, best for cut-and-come-again
- **Iceberg** — Crisp, mild; needs more space; longest to mature

## Pest Watch
Slugs love lettuce. Use iron phosphate bait or create barriers with copper tape. Row cover protects from early-season flea beetles.
