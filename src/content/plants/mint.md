Mint is the ultimate "plant it and forget it" herb — almost aggressively productive once established. A single plant purchased for $3 can provide years of harvests worth hundreds of dollars in grocery store equivalent. The warning: **always grow mint in a container** unless you want it to take over your entire garden.

## Growing Tips

Start with a transplant rather than seeds (mint seeds are slow and variable). Plant in a container or sink a pot into the ground to contain the spreading roots. Place where it gets morning sun and afternoon shade for best flavor.

### Container Growing
A 12-inch pot provides more than enough mint for most households. Use a rich, moisture-retaining potting mix. Water regularly — mint prefers consistently moist soil. Repot every 2–3 years to refresh the soil and roots.

### Pruning
Pinch or cut back stems regularly, especially before blooming. This keeps the plant bushy and the leaves at their most flavorful. Once mint flowers, flavor compounds in the leaves decrease.

## Harvest
Harvest individual stems by cutting just above a leaf node. The plant will branch from that point, doubling its productivity. Morning harvest after dew dries gives the most concentrated flavor.

## Preservation
- **Fresh:** Wrap in a damp paper towel, store in a zip-lock bag in the refrigerator — lasts 1–2 weeks
- **Dried:** Hang bunches upside down in a warm, airy spot for 1–2 weeks; store in an airtight container
- **Frozen:** Blanch briefly, pat dry, freeze on a baking sheet, then transfer to bags

## Varieties
- **Peppermint** — Strongest menthol flavor, excellent for tea and desserts
- **Spearmint** — Milder, sweeter; best for savory dishes and cocktails
- **Apple Mint** — Fruity, mild; great for smoothies and fruit salads
- **Chocolate Mint** — Mild chocolate-peppermint flavor; novelty but genuinely delicious

## Companion Planting Benefits
Mint's strong scent deters aphids, ants, flea beetles, and whiteflies. Plant near brassicas (cabbage, broccoli) to repel cabbage moths. Even cut mint left between plants can deter pests.
